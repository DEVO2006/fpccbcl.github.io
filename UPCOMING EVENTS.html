1.FORT PORTAL CITY MARATHON

  Run For a Purpose

  The Fort Portal City Marathon is an exciting event designed to celebrate the beauty, culture, and vibrancy of one of the most iconic tourist destinations in the world. Set against the

   backdrop of stunning landscapes, historic

  landmarks, and bustling city life, this marathon offers participants an unforgettable experience that blends fitness and healthy lifestyle, adventure, and culture.

   The Fort Portal City Marathon is poised to become a signature annual event that blends the excitement of sports with the allure of a world-class tourist destination. Whether you are

   an elite athlete, a casual runner, or a curious

  traveller, this marathon offers something for everyone—a chance to challenge yourself while exploring the beauty, culture, and charm of Fort Portal the home of the most beautiful

   and humble people.

   Join us for an unforgettable experience that celebrates health, community, and the spirit of adventure!

 The event seeks to:

   Develop the running sport and encourage sport tourism

   Promote Fort Portal City as a top destination for tourism and sports.

   Encourage health, wellness, and an active lifestyle among participants.

   Provide a unique platform for showcasing the city’s scenic routes, cultural landmarks, and tourist attractions.

   Foster community spirit and economic growth by bringing in visitors and promoting local businesses.

 ROUTES TO BE TAKEN

  The routes are designed with both runners and spectators in mind, offering plenty of viewing spots for locals and tourists to cheer on participants while enjoying the city’s sights.

 2.TUSKER LITE RWENZORI MARATHON

Our marathon distance is (as far as we know) the only marathon in the world that crosses the Equator, offering runners an unforgettable experience.

Participants will embark on a remarkable journey through the awe-inspiring Queen Elizabeth National Park, starting at the Equator and making your way towards Kasese town, where you will venture into the foothills of the breathtaking Rwenzori Mountains. This route showcases the stunning landscapes and rich biodiversity that make Uganda a truly remarkable destination.

The route was internationally certified by the World Athletics Federation on April 2, 2024.

  • Distance: 42 km (42.2 km)
  • Max elevation: 1230 m
  • Elevation gain: 720 m
  • Terrain: Tarmac

On June 26, 1900, the Toro Agreement was signed between Omukama (King) Daudi Kasagama Kyebambe IV and his chiefs, and Sir Henry Hamilton Johnstone, the H.M. Special Commissioner and Commander-in-Chief for the Protectorate of Uganda. Under Clause 2 of the agreement, Fort Portal was established as the capital of the Kingdom of Toro. The agreement also granted Fort Portal authority over the administration of the Congo Free State, which at that time included North Kivu, along with major towns such as Bunia, Butembo, and Beni.

Later, between 1956 and 1958, the British invited Toro to the Uganda Conference in Lancaster, England, to discuss the formation and independence of Uganda. Toro formally signed the agreement to join Uganda on September 20, 1962.

Within Uganda, Fort Portal served as the administrative center for districts including Kabarole, Bunyangabu, Kyenjojo, Kyegegwa, Kitagwenda, Kamwenge, Kasese, Budibugyo, and Ntoroko—areas that the Fort Portal City Chamber of Business and Commerce (FPCCBC) actively represents.
